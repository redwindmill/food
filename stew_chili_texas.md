WIP:: TEXAS CHILI (GOULASH LIKE?)
-----------

**CHILI POWDER (1 TBSP)**
- 2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper

**DRY INGREDIENTS**
- 3 tbsp chili powder
- 1 1/2 tsp dry oregano
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp garlic powder

**FRY**
- 2lbs beef chuck cubed to 1/2in
- 4 to 5 tbsp butter
- 1/4 tsp lawrys salt or table salt
- 1 to 1 1/2 cup diced yellow onion
- 2 tbsp fresh garlic minced or pressed

**WET**
- 2 tbsp ketchup
- 3 cups beef broth or water
- 1 bay leaf

PREP (FRY):
- Salt meat to taste (around 1 to 1 1/2 tsp); set to side
- Add butter to pot over medium heat
- Fry garlic in butter
- Add in onion and lawrys salt, cook for about 5 min (don't burn)
- Sear meat in pot in batches while browning

STEW (WET + DRY)
- In same pan, add broth, ketchup and a bay leaf
- While stirring in add in Dry mix, bring to a boil
- Reduce heat to a simmer
- Cook for at least an and a half or until meat is tender
- Add water if needed to prevent chili from burning
