# SMOKED ST. LOUIS RIBS

GOLDEN RULE: 1/3 salts, 1/3 sugars, 1/3 fragrant spices.

## MEAT
- St. Louis style ribs; trim hanging fat and meat

## RUB
- 8 tbsp pepper
- 4 tbsp salt
- 4 tbsp Lawry's salt
- 1 tbsp garlic powder

## OTHER
- apple cider vinegar (50%)
- pork lard

## PREP
1. Trim any hanging fat and excess meat from the St. Louis style ribs.
2. Score the membrane on the back of the ribs instead of removing it completely.
3. Place 2 heaping tablespoons of pork lard per rack into a metal pan.
4. Apply the rub evenly over all surfaces of the ribs.
5. Let the seasoned ribs sit at room temperature for 1-2 hours, or refrigerate overnight.

## SMOKING
1. Smoke the ribs membrane-side down at 225°F for 4 hours.
2. Increase the smoker temperature to 275°F and continue smoking for 1 additional hour.
3. Keep a metal pan of pork lard in the smoker for the entire smoking duration to add moisture and flavor.
4. Beginning at the 3-hour mark, spray the ribs with the apple cider vinegar mixture every 15 minutes.

## COOKING
1. Apply smoked lard to the ribs, then wrap each rack tightly in butcher paper with the meat side facing down.
2. Spray a small amount of vinegar inside the wrap to create steam.
3. Return to the smoker and cook for 30-60 minutes until a probe slides through the meat with the texture of warm butter.

## GLAZE (OPTIONAL)
1. Mix apple cider vinegar with your favorite BBQ sauce to create a thin glaze.
2. Smoke the wrapped ribs for an additional period until the glaze becomes sticky and set.
